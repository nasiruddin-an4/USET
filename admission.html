Begin Your Journey at USET

USET welcomes motivated students who are eager to gain industry-relevant skills and knowledge.

Our admission process is designed to identify students who demonstrate academic potential, a passion for learning, and a commitment to professional development.

We look beyond just test scores and grades to find students who will thrive in our practice-oriented learning environment and contribute to our university community.

Admission Requirements

What you need to apply to USET's programs

Undergraduate Programs

  • Educational Qualification

    Successfully completed HSC/A-Level or equivalent with minimum GPA of 3.5

  • Required Documents

    Completed application form, academic transcripts, copy of national ID/passport, passport-sized photographs

  • Admission Test

    Written test covering general knowledge, English, and subject-specific knowledge

  • Interview

    Personal interview to assess motivation, goals, and communication skills

Graduate Programs

  • Educational Qualification

    Bachelor's degree in a relevant field with minimum CGPA of 3.0 or equivalent

  • Required Documents

    Completed application form, all academic transcripts, CV/resume, statement of purpose, two recommendation letters

  • Professional Experience

    Minimum 1-2 years of professional experience preferred for some programs

  • Interview

    In-depth interview to assess professional goals, research interests, and suitability for the program

Application Process

Follow these steps to apply to USET

1

Choose Your Program

Review our academic programs and select the one that aligns with your interests and career goals. Make sure to check the specific admission requirements for your chosen program.

Browse Programs
2

Complete the Application Form

Fill out the online application form or download a PDF version to complete manually. Be sure to provide accurate information and contact details.

Online Application Download PDF Form
3

Submit Required Documents

Gather and submit all required documents, including academic transcripts, certificates, and identification. Make sure to follow the document submission guidelines provided.

Document Checklist
4

Pay Application Fee

Pay the non-refundable application fee. Payment can be made online through our secure payment portal or at designated banks. The application fee is BDT 1,000 for undergraduate programs and BDT 1,500 for graduate programs.

Payment Options
5

Take the Admission Test

If your application is accepted, you'll be invited to take an admission test. The test format and content vary depending on the program you're applying to. Prepare using our sample questions and study guide.

Test Preparation Guide
6

Attend the Interview

Shortlisted candidates will be invited for a personal interview. This is an opportunity for us to get to know you better and for you to ask questions about the program and university.

7

Receive Admission Decision

Admission decisions are typically communicated within 2-3 weeks after the interview. If accepted, you'll receive an admission letter with instructions for enrollment.

Scholarships & Financial Aid

USET offers various scholarships and financial aid options to support deserving students.

Merit-Based Scholarships

Awarded to students with outstanding academic achievements:

  • Chancellor's Scholarship: 100% tuition waiver for students with exceptional academic records
  • Academic Excellence Scholarship: 50% tuition waiver for top performers
  • Dean's Scholarship: 25% tuition waiver for high-achieving students

Need-Based Financial Aid

Available to students who demonstrate financial need:

  • USET Access Grant: Up to 75% tuition assistance based on financial need
  • Work-Study Program: On-campus employment opportunities to help with educational expenses
  • Deferred Payment Plan: Option to pay tuition fees in installments

Special Category Scholarships

Designed to promote diversity and support underrepresented groups:

  • Women in STEM Scholarship: For female students pursuing programs in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics
  • Rural Development Scholarship: For students from underserved rural areas
  • Differently-Abled Student Grant: Financial support for students with disabilities

How to Apply for Scholarships

Scholarship applications are considered as part of the admission process. No separate application is required for most scholarships. For need-based financial aid, additional documentation may be requested to verify financial status.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the admission process

USET accepts applications for fall and spring semesters. The application deadlines are June 30 for the fall semester and November 30 for the spring semester. However, we recommend applying early as some programs have limited seats.

The admission test typically includes multiple-choice questions and a written component. For undergraduate programs, the test covers English, mathematics, general knowledge, and subject-specific areas relevant to your chosen program. For graduate programs, the test focuses on specialized knowledge in your field of study. Sample questions and preparation guides are available on our website.

Yes, USET welcomes applications from international students. In addition to the standard requirements, international applicants must provide proof of English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL), a copy of their passport, and may need to have their academic credentials evaluated by a recognized agency. International students should apply at least 3-4 months before the semester starts to allow time for visa processing.

USET has limited on-campus housing facilities available on a first-come, first-served basis. Priority is given to students from outside Dhaka and international students. The university also maintains a list of approved off-campus housing options and can assist students in finding suitable accommodation near the campus.

Yes, USET accepts transfer credits from accredited institutions. Transfer students must provide official transcripts and course descriptions for evaluation. Generally, courses with a grade of C or better may be considered for transfer credit. A maximum of 60 credit hours can be transferred for undergraduate programs, and up to 9 credit hours for graduate programs.
